THE MACHINE THAT GOES PING

ABOUT

The marvelous MACHINE THAT GOES PING now right in your browser with not one, but two marvelous ping making engines!

Press PLAY and lose yourself in sonic soundscapes created specifically for your dancing pleasure.



We (the IT CAME FROM OUTER LET team) built this awesome sound machine in 48 hours during Clojure Cup 2015 competition. It's written completely from scratch in a language called ClojureScript and makes good use of Web Audio API and SVG.

This is a post-contest version - we've made several fixes and adjustments since winning 2nd place.

INSTRUCTIONS

  • There are two synthesizers and corresponding sequencer tracks, 16 notes each by default. You can adjust the number with STEPS control.
  • Top track controls synthesizer A.
  • Bottom track controls the synthesizer B.
  • White buttons on sequencer steps toggle notes on or off.
  • Use knobs on sequencer steps to set pitch for individual notes.
  • Synthesizers are synthesizers - just play with the knobs! Easy on master volume though...
  • SAVE will save your work, you can share it with friends by sharing the URL of the browser window.
  • PLAY starts the sequencers! You want to press it.
  • PAUSE stops the music! You don't ever want to press it.
  • RAND. NOTES picks random notes so you don't have to!
